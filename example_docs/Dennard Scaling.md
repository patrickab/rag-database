Principle in semiconductor manufacturing 

- as transistors shrink, their power density remains constant
	- voltage and current are scaled down proportionally to length
	- Faster, more power-efficient chips by doubling the transistor count without increasing power consumption.

Since 2004 Dennart Scaling has largely stagnated

This has primarily been caused by **current leakage**
- current leakage is energy lost to resistence of transistor material

This resulted in inability to consistently lower the **supply voltage** as transistors shrank.
